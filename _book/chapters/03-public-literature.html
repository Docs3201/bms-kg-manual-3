4  Public Literature Evidence

What published studies say about genes, proteins, and productivity

4.1 Overview

The KG integrates cause-effect relationships curated from ~500 publications on genetic perturbation in the context of CHO productivity. This chapter covers how to query that evidence layer.

4.2 How Literature Evidence is Structured

Publications were scouted from ~45,000 CHO-related papers through a two-stage filter:

  1. Filter 1 — grouped into three categories:
    • (A) Gene/protein/metabolite target manipulation and cell engineering studies ✓ included
    • (B) Bioprocess optimisation
    • (C) Other studies
  2. Filter 2 — Inclusion: productivity insights through target manipulation or omics-driven cell-line engineering. Exclusion: not directly relevant to target manipulation impacting productivity.

Result: ~500 publications describing genetic interventions (overexpression, knockdown, knockout) and their effects on productivity.

Each extracted relationship is stored as a directed edge with properties:

Property Description
cause Gene / protein / metabolite manipulated
manipulation OE (overexpression), KD (knockdown), KO (knockout)
effect_entity Productivity phenotype affected
effect_direction Positive (+) or negative (−)
cell_line e.g., CHO-K1 GS KO
target_product e.g., IgG1
reactor e.g., Fed-batch
pmid PubMed ID of source publication
evidence_sentence Source text from the paper

4.3 Key Effect Entities

The KG tracks effects across four biological dimensions:

Productivity: specific_productivity_qp · product_titer · volumetric_productivity · secretion_rate · product_yield · production_stability

Growth: viable_cell_density_vcd · cell_viability · IVCD · specific_growth_rate · apoptosis_rate · cell_cycle_phase_distribution · cloning_efficiency

Metabolic: lactate_production/consumption · ammonia_production · glucose_consumption · glutamine_metabolism · redox_balance · metabolic_flux · lipid_metabolism

Quality: glycosylation_profile · fucosylation · sialylation · product_purity · protein_fragmentation · aggregation_level · host_cell_protein_content

4.5 Worked Example: HSPA5 (GRP78/BiP)

Literature

HSPA5 encodes the ER chaperone GRP78/BiP. Published manipulation studies support overexpression as a productivity lever:

PMID Manipulation Effect Product
32624757 GRP78 OE ↑ Titre mAb
24081924 BiP co-expression ↑ qP Recombinant protein
1373378 GRP78 OE ↑ Productivity IgG

Mechanism: UPR activation expands ER folding capacity → improves secretory efficiency.

4.6 Worked Example: ST14

Literature

ST14 encodes Matriptase, a serine protease. Knockout studies show improved product quality:

PMID Manipulation Effect
29777593 ST14 KO ↓ Protein fragmentation
29777593 ST14 KO ↑ Product purity

Mechanism: Matriptase degrades secreted product → knockout eliminates fragmentation and improves purity.
