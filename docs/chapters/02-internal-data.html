3.4 C · Co-expression Networks (WGCNA)

What is WGCNA?

Weighted Gene Co-expression Network Analysis (WGCNA) clusters features that vary together across samples into modules. Each module is summarised by its Module Eigengene (ME)—the first principal component of all features in that module—which is then correlated with bioprocess traits (qP, titre, IVCD).

1
Correlate — pairwise correlations across 36 samples × 3 omics layers
2
Weight — raise correlations to soft-threshold power β (amplifies strong connections, suppresses noise; β=19 genes, β=6 protein, β=14 metabolites)
3
Cluster — build Topological Overlap Matrix (TOM) → hierarchical clustering → 20 gene, 5 protein, 5 metabolite modules
4
Summarise — reduce each module to its ME (1 value per sample)
5
Correlate to traits — Pearson r of each ME vs. qP, titre, IVCD

Published precedent: Melville et al. (2011, Journal of Biotechnology) applied WGCNA to 295 microarrays from 121 CHO cultures and identified six transcriptional modules, five of which correlated with bioprocess variables including qP. The BMS KG replicates and extends this approach across all three omics layers.

3.5 D · Multi-Omics Factor Analysis (MOFA)

What is MOFA?

Multi-Omics Factor Analysis (MOFA) is an unsupervised dimensionality-reduction method that integrates transcriptomics, proteomics, and metabolomics simultaneously to uncover latent factors—hidden biological programs that explain variation across samples.

1
Input preparation — Top 5,000 variable features (transcriptomics & proteomics), all 596 metabolite features
2
MOFA decomposition — Joint matrix factorisation across 3 omics views; K=10 latent factors (sufficient to capture major sources without over-fragmenting biology)
3
Factor–trait correlation — Spearman's ρ of each factor score vs. qP, titre, IVCD across 36 samples (Spearman is robust to outliers and non-linear monotonic relationships)
4
Feature weight interpretation — top 80th percentile features per factor per omics layer extracted for pathway enrichment

Significant Factors and Their Biological Meaning

Factor Correlation pattern Interpretation
Factor 1 qP +0.848 · IVCD +0.860 · Titre +0.904 Full-spectrum productivity signal — higher cell efficiency, volumetric yield, and cumulative biomass
Factor 2 qP positive · IVCD negative High-efficiency-per-cell phenotype — not driven by biomass accumulation
Factor 3 Proteomics-only Post-translational regulatory axis decoupled from transcription
Factor 4 Transcriptomics-only Transcriptional program not yet reflected in protein or metabolites at sampled time points
Factor 5 Titre & qP strongly negative Pure metabolic suppressor acting on secretory machinery
Important

Factor 1 is the most actionable factor. Genes / proteins / metabolites with high positive Factor 1 weights are pro-productivity candidates; those with high negative weights are anti-productivity candidates.
